\section{Limitations and Ethical Considerations}
\label{sec:limits-ethics}

\textbf{Limitations.} First, our minimal ReAct harness is controlled but not representative of production code-interpreter or AutoGen-style systems; the harness ablation confirms that failure distributions change with scaffolding. Second, 38.1\% of the original runs are infrastructure failures, and several model--suite cells have no valid coverage; these cells must not be interpreted as model inability. Third, the final 150-trace gold set observes four of five taxonomy stages: Output-Mismatch (77), Runtime (39), Answer-Error (22), and Code-Generation (12), while Analytical-Plan has no support. The newly annotated 103-trace tranche has modest raw pre-adjudication stage agreement ($\kappa=.350$, 95\% CI [.235, .447]) despite perfect within-annotator QC replication; this reflects a systematic boundary dispute resolved through consensus, not strong innate class separability. Fourth, the revised classifier is oracle-assisted because it compares traces with the reference solution path. Its 91.3\% final-gold accuracy should not be interpreted as trace-only deployment performance, and its rules were refined after inspecting the gold errors; independent held-out validation is required. Finally, model aliases and the third-party API are not fully reproducible, and the remaining automatically labeled traces inherit classifier noise.

\textbf{Ethical considerations.} No personal data are used; the tasks derive from public UCI and DSBench/ModelOff resources or synthetic generators. The third-party endpoint may log prompts, so users should not submit sensitive data. Closed-model, English-language, and Python-only coverage limits generalisability. Infrastructure failures are reported separately to prevent misleading model rankings. Public tasks and fixed synthetic seeds permit contamination or benchmark gaming; diagnostic metrics should therefore accompany raw success rates.

\textbf{Generative AI usage.} LLMs are experimental subjects and, in legacy analyses, auxiliary judges. The final 150 labels are human adjudications; LLM pre-labels are not treated as gold.
