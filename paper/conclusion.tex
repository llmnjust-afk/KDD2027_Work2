\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conc}

AgentFail provides full-trace diagnosis for data-science LLM agents, separating five failure stages from the independent question of whether a failure is observable. Across 5{,}984 runs, silent-failure rates remain strongly task-dependent and feedback recovers silent failures substantially less often than loud failures. The final 150-trace human gold set contains 77 Output-Mismatch, 39 Runtime, 22 Answer-Error, and 12 Code-Generation cases; Analytical-Plan remains uninstantiated. Raw stage agreement on the newly annotated tranche is modest ($\kappa=.350$), underscoring taxonomy difficulty, while consensus adjudication supports a revised oracle-assisted classifier with 91.3\% accuracy and 90.3\% macro-F1 over observed classes. Oracle repair matching its no-op control limits causal claims. Future work should validate the classifier on an independent gold set, populate Analytical-Plan naturally, and test production agent harnesses.
